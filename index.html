Purpose

About

The Midwest Regional meeting serves as an opportunity for Midwest physician scientists and trainees at all levels to come together to share scientific discovery and build community.

Target Audience

Attendees from all physician scientist career levels and degree backgrounds are welcome to attend! (Undergraduate, health-science students, medical and dual-degree students, postdoctoral students, residents, fellows, and attending physicians).

Breakout sessions will be targeted at early-career trainees.

Speakers

Keynote Speaker

Robert Baiocchi, MD, PhD

The Ohio State University

Dr. Robert Baiocchi's journey from aspiring primary care physician to pioneering cancer researcher began in 1990 at Roswell Park Comprehensive Cancer Institute. His transformative encounters with immune-compromised patients affected by EBV-driven malignancies sparked a passion for translational science that shaped his entire career. Now a Professor of Internal Medicine and Director of the Physician Scientist Training Programs in the Department of Medicine and College of Medicine at The Ohio State University, Dr. Baiocchi describes his path as "incredibly rewarding." As institutional PI for the NIH AIDS Malignancy Consortium and Section Head of the Lymphoma Disease Group, he dedicates himself to mentoring the next generation of physician scientists while advancing novel therapeutic strategies for patients with cancer and other inflammatory diseases. His laboratory bridges fundamental research with clinical applications, focusing on the epigenetics of B cell transformation, lymphoma therapeutics, and immune surveillance.

Keynote Speaker

Chi Van Dang, MD, PhD

Johns Hopkins

From his early life as one of ten children in Saigon, Vietnam, to his current role as CEO & Scientific Director of the Ludwig Institute for Cancer Research and Bloomberg Distinguished Professor of Cancer Medicine at Johns Hopkins, Dr. Chi Van Dang's scientific journey reflects a profound dedication to understanding and fighting cancer. Following in the footsteps of his father, Vietnam's first neurosurgeon, Dr. Dang has pioneered groundbreaking research into how cancer cells utilize energy, making fundamental discoveries about the MYC gene's role in cellular metabolism. His innovative work spans leadership positions at Johns Hopkins University and the University of Pennsylvania's Abramson Cancer Center. As former Editor-in-Chief of Cancer Research and a member of the National Academy of Medicine, Dr. Dang continues to advance our understanding of cancer metabolism and champion promising new approaches like chronotherapy and dietary modulation.

Keynote Speaker

Deena Chisolm, PhD

Nationwide Children's Hospital

Dr. Deena Chisolm is the Nationwide Foundation Endowed Chair in Health Equity Research and Professor of Pediatrics and Public Health at The Ohio State University. She is the Director of the Center for Child Health Equity and Outcomes Research and VicePresident for Health Services Research in the Abigail Wexner Research Institute at Nationwide Children’s Hospital. Her research focuses on improving health and healthcare for children and families, particularly those from minoritized and socially disadvantaged populations. She has over 120 peer-reviewed publications and nearly 20 years of continuous federal grant funding. Her current projects explore how healthcare delivery systems, health policies, and social drivers of health influence healthcare accesses and health outcomes. Dr. Chisolm prioritizes bringing her research findings and her methodological expertise into the government sector. This has included being appointed to serve on the CMS Federal Advisory Council on Outreach and Education and the Governor’s COVID-19 Strike Force. She is the former chairman of the Ohio Commission on Minority Health Board and currently serves as board chairman of the Health Policy Institute of Ohio Board and the Vice-president pro tem of the Columbus Board of Health.
